The history of Indian temples dates back to around the 2nd century BCE, evolving from simple cave shrines into magnificent stone structures over centuries. Early temples were built during the Maurya and Gupta periods, followed by grand architectural styles like Nagara (North India), Dravidian (South India), and Vesara (Deccan region). These temples were not only places of worship but also centers of education, art, music, and culture. Over time, dynasties like the Cholas, Pallavas, Hoysalas, and Mughals influenced temple design and construction. The Kedarnath Temple is one of the most sacred Hindu shrines, located in the majestic Himalayas in Rudraprayag district, Uttarakhand. Dedicated to Lord Shiva, it is part of the Char Dham Yatra and one of the twelve Jyotirlingas of India. Surrounded by snow-capped peaks and the Mandakini River, Kedarnath offers a deeply spiritual and serene experience. Despite harsh weather conditions, it attracts thousands of pilgrims every year.
